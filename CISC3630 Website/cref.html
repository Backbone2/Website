Chord Type Definitions

Root - Chords are built off of a single note called the root. It can be identified by the first letter of the Chord.

*Ex. Cmin has a root of C. Dmaj has a root of D.*

Major - Notated as "maj" (but is usually omitted). Take the Root, count up 4 semitones from the root, and then 7 semitones from the root.

*Ex. Dmaj is D, F♯ and A*

Minor - Notated as "min" or "m". Take the Root, count up 3 semitones from the root, and then 7 semitones from the root.

*Ex. Emin is E, G and B*

Diminished - Notated as "dim". Take the Root, count up 3 semitones from the root, and then 6 semitones from the root.

*Ex. Gdim is G, B♭ and D♭*
